More About D.A.R.T.A.

The field of archaeology has long posed challenges for professionals with disabilities due to its physically demanding nature. Archaeologists often spend weeks or months excavating, with less than 5% of a dig site typically containing artifacts. This leads to delays and increased costs. Approximately 15% of archaeologists have mobility and physical disabilities, and uneven, remote, and hazardous dig sites further hinder participation.

To address these issues, we developed Project D.A.R.T.A. (Dual-frequency Artifact Recognition and Terrain Analysis), a drone equipped with Ground Penetrating Radar (GPR). This technology allows for rapid surveys of large or hard-to-reach areas, reducing time and labor costs. By overlaying GPR data and 3D models onto real-world views, researchers can quickly gain context to make informed excavation decisions.

Mixed Reality glasses allow archaeologists and experts to see excavation data overlaid directly onto the real world, guiding digs in real time from anywhere. Instead of archaeology being defined by physical endurance, MR shifts the focus to collaboration and inclusivity, opening the field to diverse talents. With immersive overlays and remote connectivity, specialists can interpret scans, direct drones, and share insights instantly — transforming archaeology into a technology-driven discipline where everyone can participate, regardless of location or mobility.

Aerial Exploration

Larger drones with extended battery life for surveying hard-to-reach areas

Project D.A.R.T.A. uses powerful drones designed to fly over uneven, rocky, and hazardous terrain where traditional GPR carts cannot go. With extended battery life and larger payload capacity, these drones expand the reach of archaeologists, enabling surveys in areas that were previously inaccessible. By overcoming physical barriers, drones make exploration faster, safer, and more inclusive.

Ground-Penetrating Radar

Advanced radar capable of deep scans and high-resolution imaging of underground structures

At the heart of D.A.R.T.A. is dual-frequency Ground-Penetrating Radar. This advanced system can switch between deep scanning to detect buried structures and high-resolution scanning to identify fine details. Compared to traditional cart-based GPR, drone-mounted dual-frequency radar improves accuracy and efficiency, reducing wasted excavation time and helping archaeologists pinpoint promising dig sites more quickly.

Immersive MR Visualization

Immersive overlays and voice commands for guiding digs remotely and inclusively

Mixed Reality glasses transform how archaeologists interact with excavation data. By overlaying GPR scans and 3D models directly onto the real world, specialists can guide digs in real time, even from remote locations. Voice commands and live audio make the system accessible to archaeologists with disabilities, shifting archaeology from a test of physical endurance to a field defined by inclusivity and collaboration

Smart AI Analysis

Instant analysis of scan data to identify artifacts and reduce wasted excavation time

Artificial intelligence software processes GPR data instantly, identifying potential artifacts and structures with remarkable speed. This reduces the guesswork and wasted effort of traditional excavation, allowing archaeologists to focus on areas with the highest potential. By accelerating site selection, AI helps researchers uncover history faster and more efficiently

Magnetic Charging Stations

Modular charging pads that enable longer missions with minimal downtime in the field

D.A.R.T.A. includes modular magnetic charging stations that keep drones powered for extended missions. These stations reduce downtime in the field, ensuring that surveys can continue without interruption. By supporting longer flight times and quick recharging, the system is built for real-world archaeological challenges where time and efficiency are critical

Open Archaeology

Remote participation tools that break barriers and open archaeology to diverse talents

Accessibility is at the core of Project D.A.R.T.A. Archaeologists with mobility or physical disabilities can now participate fully in digs by directing drones remotely, interpreting scans through MR glasses, and collaborating in real time. By removing physical barriers, D.A.R.T.A. empowers diverse voices to contribute to the discovery and preservation of human history, making archaeology stronger and more inclusive.
